Introduction:

The goal of this lab was to figure out how hight we needed to make the descent of a hot wheel roller coaster in order for the little car to successfully loop a loop. We used our knowledge of kinematics and new information about conservation of energy in order to attempt and prove our hypothesis that the car will go further when the loop de loop is not part of its journey.

Pertinent Formulas/Laws:

Etot,initial = Etot,final
PEi + KEi= PEf + KEf
*Beginning at rest, KEi = 0*
mgh1 = mgh2 + 1/2mv2
v2 = gr
Fc = mv2/r
Fc = mg
mg = mv2/r
mgh1 = mgh2 + 1/2mv2
mgh1 = mgh2 + 1/2m(gr)
h1 = 2r + 1/2 (r)
h2 = 2r
h1 = 2.5(r)

In addition, you will need the formula from projectile motion:
x = (vlaunch*cos) * t
And:
= tan-1(height of ramp/length of base of ramp)

Materials:

  • hot wheels set
  • metric ruler
  • Stopwatch

Procedure:

  1. Split into groups of 3-5
  2. Set up the Hot Wheels set such that there is one loop-de-loop, which leads into a ramp from which the car will jump
  3. Find the minimum height your car will need to begin at in order to make it around the loop you have constructed, using Conservation of Energy and circular motion (as outlined in the formulae above)
  4. Find the velocity your car will be going at the bottom of the loop-the-loop setup, assuming you start from the height you calculated in step #3
  5. See if your predicted height will allow the care to travel around the loop. If not, raise the starting height until the car does make it around the loop safely. Record your actual starting height here, along with the angle of the ramp off of which the car will be jumping
  6. Find the velocity your car will be going at the bottom of this new loop-the-loop setup, assuming you start from the height you measured in step #5
  7. Let the car go down the descent, through the loop-de-loop, and then jump off the ramp. Measure how far away from the base of the Hot Wheels set the car lands, as well as the time it takes to travel during the jump(10 times)
  8. Find the average distance/time the car traveled
  9. Find the initial velocity the car had while jumping off the ramp, using the equation of projectile motion above.
  10. Set up a simpler Hot Wheels construction, such that the car begins from the same height at which you started for the loop the loop setup, and then jumps off a ramp of the same length, and raised at the same angle. Now, however, just let the car descend and jump.
  11. Repeat steps #7 - 10
